How we met

Jodi was an excited, first-year student in Pretoria and looking for some second-hand textbooks for University. Out of all the posters advertising textbooks for sale on campus, she chose mine and called me. I was on my way to Sugar Bay in Ballito and remember feeling very relieved that I could spend the next two weeks in peace and not stress about who was going to buy my old books. It was in these two weeks I came to know Jodi’s passionate and determined nature, as I lost my phone and she could get hold of me. When I found my phone I had multiple missed calls and panicked text messages about how I was a scam-artist for accepting money but not delivering books. I was humoured but felt bad and as soon as I got back to Pretoria I organised to meet her. I remember my sister, Shannon, mentioning that I may be meeting my soul-mate in jest, before I left the house. What was supposed to be a simple, “business deal,” ended up being a three-hour chat on the side of Lynnwood road. We both felt the chemistry and what started off as study sessions for test week turned into a blooming romance.

Those were some magical textbooks!
